Crops & Farming

What grows in Wai

Agriculture of Wai

Kharif · Cereal

Rice

Grown during the monsoon season on low-lying fields with adequate water retention. Staple crop for local consumption and regional markets.

Jun – Nov Water-intensive

Annual · Cash crop

Sugarcane

The backbone of Wai's agricultural economy. Grown on large landholdings and supplied to nearby sugar mills.

Year-round Cash crop

Seasonal · Horticulture

Flowering crops

Marigold, jasmine, and other flowers cultivated for local mandis and religious markets.High-value crop popular among smallholders.

Oct – Mar High value Mandi sold

Zaid · Fruit

Grapes

Table and wine grapes grown in the drier western belt of Wai. Grape vineyards supply Pune and Nashik markets. Growing export potential.

Dec – Apr Export quality

Rabi · Cereal

Jowar

Traditional dryland crop grown on upland black soil. Drought-tolerant and important for food security.

Oct – Feb Dryland Fodder

Year-round · Vegetables

Seasonal vegetables

Tomato, onion, and leafy greens grown by smallholders near the village. Sold at Pune APMC and local weekly markets.

All seasons Local market

Market & Mandi

Where Wai
sells its harvest

Primary market

APMC Pune (Gultekdi)

Maharashtra's largest agricultural market. ~35 km from Wai via NH-65. Open Monday–Saturday, 6 AM – 6 PM. Handles fruits, vegetables, grains, and flowers.

Get directions →

Local weekly market

Wai Gram Bazaar

Weekly market held every Thursday at the village centre. Ideal for vegetables, flowers, and small quantities of grain. No transport cost for local farmers.

Soil & Water

The Water
Resources of Wai

Water sources

🏞️

Mula-Mutha River

Primary surface water source. Used for irrigation via lift schemes and canal networks. Flow is seasonal — strongest June–October.

💧

Farm ponds

farm ponds constructed under state schemes. Store monsoon runoff for dry-season irrigation of vegetables and flowers.

🌊

Drip irrigation

Drip and sprinkler systems used widely in grape vineyards and sugarcane fields. Subsidised under Maharashtra's micro-irrigation programme.

Get your Soil Health Card

Every farmer in Wai is entitled to a free Soil Health Card from the government. It tells you exactly which nutrients your soil needs — and saves money on fertilisers. Collect yours at the GP office or apply online.
